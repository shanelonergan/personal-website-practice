Joan Rosenfels

Acting Studio

Cultivating working actors and creative artists

Joan draws on a variety of acting methods to encourage the actor to live out the story of the play. Employing physical techniques such as Alexander work, there’s a focus on the body as the conduit for expression. Students learn to “start where they are” and to bring their fullest and most present selves to the work.

About Joan

Joan currently teaches ongoing classes to and coaches professional actors for TV, film and theater. Her students have been nominated and won Tony awards. Her students have starred in many Broadway shows including: American Idiot, West Side Story, Million Dollar Quartet, Next to Normal, A Little Night Music, Mama Mia, Thoroughly Modern Millie-(Tony Award), Moving Out, Who's Afraid of Virginia Wolf-(Tony nomination), Gypsy, etc. Joan coached on the film, Sex and the City and has coached actors for Ugly Betty, Law and Order, 30 Rock, and an upcoming Adam Sandler film. She has taught on the faculty of Barnard College, Rutgers University, Ensemble Studio Theater, and the Fieldston School. Joan has taught playmaking workshops at the University of Massachusetts, Harvard Divinity School, and Lincoln Center Institute, at homeless shelters, Mount Sinai Hospital and in many public schools throughout the five boroughs. As an actress, Joan can be seen on all the Law and Orders, Sex and the City, Conviction, Johnny Zero. Theaters she has performed at include: Manhattan Theater Club, Roundabout Theater, Signature Theater, Ensemble Studio Theater, Ontological Theater, The Alliance, Geva, Triad Stage, Pioneer.
